How to use Jellyfin and Jellyseer

{% endblock %} {% block main-content %}

I think Jellyfin and Jellyseer are by the far the most used services on SVU for obvious reasons. Jellyfin allows you to stream Movies, Shows and almost any kind of multi media content. So far the collection of Movies and Shows hosted on Jellyfin has been personally curated by all of us. The Jellyfin library has grown steadily and I see new requests almost every 3 or 4 days. So I think its warranted to spend a little time dispelling any misconceptions on how it works so you can use it better.


The inner workings of Jellyfin is based upon something called a "client server architecture". Similar to how the interent works, we have one or more Jellyfin servers (in our case just one) that has all the content stored and they host them to Jellyfin clients connecting to it.

So there are 3 things you essentially need to use the Jellyfin server. You need a jellyfin account which you can create for yourself by contacting me. You then require a Jellyfin client. Most operating systems including IOS and Android have an official Jellyfin client which you can download from here


The last thing you need is a stable connection to the SVU server with tailscale. When you have all three of these, you simply need to open your Jellyfin client and enter the server address 


http://100.72.58.114:8096/

Once the login process is over, you can browse our list of collections at your own leisure. You can have any number of jellyfin clients connected to the server, the only condition being that you must have tailscale installed in each of the clients. You can even directly use jellyfin with your browser by navigating to the link above, however you might not be able to play some Movies or Shows due to issues with video codecs. You might even encounter this with a jellyfin client, so if you get a "Playback error" simply use the client with an external video player like mpv or vlc. I would recommend mpv. You can instruct the jellyfin client to use the external player by navigating to the settings.


Along with Jellyfin, SVU also hosts another service called Jellyseer that lets you request content. You can navigate to the service from the homepage and use your Jellyfin credentials to login. Both of these services work parallely to keep track of the content in the server. Once you generate a request with Jellyseer, I have a telegram bot that sends a notification to a group after which I upload the content to the server. If you have access to our server via ssh, you can even upload your own Movies and Shows. Look at Introduction to SSH if you need more information.
